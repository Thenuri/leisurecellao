Yala National Park

Yala National Park is Sri Lanka's oldest & most famous national park in the country. It is the island's second-largest National Park. The appearance of leopards and free-roaming of animals including elephants passing through the jungle in their free mobility is a unique experience for all who visits this sanctuary. Yala safari is an exciting experience for all visitors to Sri Lanka. Under British authority, the park was ironically used as a hunting place for the upper crust. Yala is home to 44 different mammal species and 215 different bird species.





Udawalawa National Park

Udawalawe National Park is Sri Lanka's third most visited park due ot the existence of various ecosystems, Udawalawe is home to a diverse range of species. The elephants, on the other hand, are the main draw of Udawalawe, which provides living to a herd of 600 to 700 elephants. Apart from elephants The Udawalawe National Park is home to a so many of other Sri Lankan wildlife, including the endangered Toque Macaque and the rare Sri Lankan Leopard. Despite the fact that the Sri Lankan Sloth Bear has been documented, it is an uncommon sighting. There are a variety of rear bird species, some of which are indigenous, in addition to fish, reptiles, and amphibians.



Minneriya National Park

Minneriya National Park is home to the world's largest known gathering of Asian elephants. Currently 8,890 hectares of the park within a few square kilometres of the Minneriya Tank are home to groups of up to 350 elephants. According to available sources, there are as many as 700 elephants. Wild elephants move from Wasgamuwa National Park to Minneriya National Park in search of food and refuge during the dry season in August and September each year. Elephants are a big draw for visitors to Minneriya National Park, especially during the dry season.




Gal Oya National Park

The Gal Oya Valley project was Sri Lanka's first major irrigation project after independence. The Gal Oya National Park is Sri Lanka's only National Park where you can go on a boat safari. There are many little islands visible because it borders a reservoir. The frequent sightings of elephants swimming from one island to the next are a highlight. Elephants in Sri Lanka are excellent swimmers who use their trunks as snorkels. They can also move over water for extended distances. Indeed, it is said that the first elephants to arrive in Sri Lanka swam all the way from India and settled on the island.
