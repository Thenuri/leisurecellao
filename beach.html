Mirissa



Mirissa is the island's biggest fishing port, located on the island's southern tip. It's not difficult to get to because of its beauty and proximity to Colombo (the capital city). The airport in the capital is about a two-hour drive away. The small beach town and its wide beach are both lovely, with a dense grove of palm trees behind them. The village is situated on a little slope behind the beach.

Talalla South


Talalla South, another beach on the south coast, is beginning to see more development. Because everything is covered behind the trees, the beach, a golden ribbon of gently sloping sand, keeps its natural beauty. There is only one little snack bar on the beach, but the town contains a few restaurants and other tourist-catering companies. It's the type of beach where you'll see other tourists but a lot more locals, particularly children, playing in the waves. Small fishing boats border the shore, and parts of the beach are covered in fishermen's nets. A huge Buddhist temple is nearby if you want to go sightseeing. The beach is about an hour drive from Galle.

Nilaveli


Nilaveli Beach is a wide, sandy beach with palm trees and fishing boats dotted about. It's a 30-minute drive from the city of Trincomalee in the northeastern portion of Sri Lanka. Due to its own small reef, this tiny island features tranquil beaches with modest waves. Despite its size, Nilaveli's beach is popular with both locals and tourists. There are lifeguards on duty, as well as numerous small restaurants along the beach, the most of which specialize in seafood.
