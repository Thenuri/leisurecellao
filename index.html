Welcome To Cellao

Sri Lanka is a beautiful island in the Indian Ocean. It is located 30 kilometres from the south of India. Mountains can be found in the south-central region. The rest of the country is mostly low-lying, with flat coastal plains. Many sandy beaches and lagoons could be found throughout the country. Sri Lanka has a 3,000-year known history, with prehistoric human settlements extending back at least 125,000 years.

It has a rich cultural heritage, with the Pali Canon, Sri Lanka's early Buddhist literature, dating back to the Fourth Buddhist Council in 29 BC. From the old Silk Road to the present Maritime Silk Road, it was strategically important because of its geographic location and deep harbours. Ceylon was the name given to Sri Lanka from the beginning of British colonial control. In the early twentieth century, a nationalist political movement formed in the country in order to gain political independence, which was granted in 1948; the country became a republic in 1972 and took its current name. The recent history of Sri Lanka Many cultures, languages, and ethnicities can be found on the island. The majority of the population is Sinhalese, however, Tamils have also played a significant role in the island's history. There are also established groups of Moors, Burghers, and Malays.





Cuisines



Sri Lanka is an island of Rice & Curry as well as Island of Sea Food. Rice is the fundamental food of Sri Lanka & the traditional rice grown in the country is scientifically proven for improving health & preventing certain illnesses. Sri Lankans make use of coconut juice, a fruit abundantly available in the island to prepare incredibly delicious dishes. They add herbs & spices such as cinnamon, black pepper, goraka for which the country is historically famous for, to form the unique taste of food. Being an island surrounded by the sea, seafood plays a significant role in Srilanka cuisine.

Srilanka Kithul palm syrup Jaggary has a unique mouthwatering taste. Tea is the most important beverage in the island & it is world famous for its finest tea. Sri Lankans are also fond of drinking herbal drinks such as Belimal, Ranawara, Eramusu & kola kenda. These beverages help for longevity & healthy lifestyle of the nation. Toddy is a famous alcoholic drink made from palm tram sap, which tourist loves & enjoys. Malu Ambulthiyal, Kirikos, Polos Ambula, Kiribath with Lunumiris, Pol Sambol, Gotukola Sambol are some of the exclusive Srilankan dishes you should not avoid enjoying,





Visual, literary And Performing Arts

Characteristics of Sri Lankan arts and crafts are heavily influenced by Buddhist culture. Due to the geographical location of the island, its artistic traditions have influenced Indian form as well. Buddhist religious influence in Sri Lankan art is passed down through generations. Religious ideas are visible in various forms of art, including painting, sculpture, and architecture.

Cave and temple paintings, such as Sigiriya frescoes and religious paintings in Dambulla and Kandy Temple of the Tooth Relic, are among the most prominent features of Sri Lankan art.





Festivals


Sri Lankans celebrate the Sinhala and Tamil New Year, which is a major cultural celebration. This event takes place in April to mark the end of the harvest season. Muslims commemorate Mawlid by fasting during the Islamic month of Ramadan, although Muslims represents only 7% of the population their festivals too are celebrated with great pleasure giving due prominence to traditions.

Easter and Christmas are two of the most important Christian holidays. Esala perahare is a distinctive Sri Lankan sign with a mix of parades, colors & traditions that takes place during the Esala month. It takes place between the months of July and August. It's a Buddhist event with dances and elephants that are colorfully decorated with traditional costumes. Fire dances, whip dances, Kandyan dances, and a variety of other cultural dances are all performed.
